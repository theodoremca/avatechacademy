Frequently Asked Questions

What is Stutern?

Stutern is a skills development and job placement platform. We train and connect young African talents with long-term employment. We offer training classes that help you learn valuable skills that are relevant and valuable for the 21st century. Lastly, we also work with employers who want to hire candidates with digital skills.


What courses are offered?

We have the following courses:

  • Android Development
  • Frontend Development
  • Data Science & Machine learning
  • User Interface/User Experience Design
  • Software Development

What course is right for me?

While all the courses lead to relatively great careers, you must choose a track that aligns with your strengths, goals, and aspirations in life. Each program is immersive, having a strong reason will help you see it through successfully. Below are a few hints from us:

  • If you have an interest in design (graphic, fashion, etc.), possess any type of artistic skill (writing, photography, etc.), or love to interact with people, then, our UI/UX Design course might be ideal for you. UI/UX Designers are important in the product development phase because their work is what makes a user love a product. It borrows a lot from psychology even though the design aspect is what is more pronounced.

  • If you have some interest in design and programming but would rather spend more time on your computer than with your prospective users, then Frontend might be for you. Another way to know if the Frontend Development course is right for you is if you are the kind of person who prefers someone else to bother about the right color combination, the layout of the page, and how beautiful it should look. As a Frontend Developer, your core role is to write HTML, CSS, and JavaScript codes that will turn (i.e. render, you will hear that a lot) those user-centric and beautiful designs (ideally given to you by a UI/UX designer) into a webpage. Frontend Developers have some design skills but use it for coding web pages rather than designing prototypes.

  • Software Development skills typically take a longer time to develop than Frontend Development skills because it is a combination of both Frontend and Backend Development. A few things that can help you decide if this is the right course for you is if you have an interest in creating functional software products. Also, if you are comfortable dealing with abstract and theoretical things (you will be doing things whose result might not be visible on a webpage but through logs), then this might be the right one for you. Software Development gives you a chance to create software products that can be used by an unlimited number of people. Only a few professions give you that opportunity. It's rad!

  • If you are interested in building mobile applications and not web applications, then our Android course might be the best option for you. Our Android Development track requires that you have some knowledge of programming already. You don't need to have any work experience to join this course but some knowledge to complete the application task.

  • If you like Sherlock Holmes, Data Science might be it for you. While that sounds like a joke, it's not far from reality. To put it into context, if you have an interest or knowledge of research and numbers, then you might do well with Data Science. You should also be open to learning how to code. The course will teach you the core programming language you need to be successful in this field.

What is the duration of the program?

The program is for 16 to 24 weeks. However, group classes holds two times a week.


Application & Enrollment Questions

What is the application process like?

There are four steps to the application process:

  1. The first stage requires that you fill the free application form. This is where you let us know about your interest and motivation for the program.
  2. The second stage have two phases:
    1. The first phase requires you to respond to our soft skills questions in writing and video format. We might request for further interviews if we need more information from you.
    2. The second phase requires you to to respond to technical questions that are specific to the course you have chosen.
  3. Based on your responses from the initial stages, you will be notified of your admission status.

Does the take-home task determine my admission into the program?

Yes, it does. The task submission is mandatory to enroll. Nevertheless, we provide help to inform you of learning resources to enable you to complete the task sufficiently. Beyond the task, the other responses you provide during the application process also determine your admission into the program.


Can someone help out with my application task?

No, no one is expected to attempt the task but you. Since you are the applicant, it is best you attempt the task yourself to help you get familiar with the course and to help decide if the course is right for you.


Can I apply for the program if I’m not a graduate or if I’m a beginner?

Yes, you can. Anyone interested in learning new digital skills is welcome to apply for any of our tracks. Our curriculums are structured in a way that no previous experience is required so beginners can join the classes as well. However, the ISA Payment option is only open to graduates who have completed the mandatory NYSC program.


Must I be a Nigerian to apply

No, we've had applicants from countries like Kenya and Ghana. We also have gradutes working in other countries beyond Nigeria. We believe that knowledge and opportunities can be equally distributed.


Tuition & Income Sharing Agreement (ISA) Questions

What payment options are available?

We have three payment options to help you start your journey to getting your dream job

  1. Full payment: You get to pay the total amount of fees required at the beginning of the classes.
  2. Installment payment: You can pay the fees required over a couple of months worked out between you and the team.
  3. Income sharing agreement (ISA): At Stutern, you can launch your career with upfront tuition and then pay back a percentage of your income according to an income sharing agreement.

What if I don’t get a job immediately after graduation?

If you choose our ISA payment option, you don’t need to worry about paying Stutern back immediately. Some of us have to wait a little bit before our dream is realized. You have a five-year window before your financial obligation with Stutern is terminated for good. However, we expect that you keep improving your skills while you wait. We will be around to help you.


What if I don’t get a job through Stutern?

We are not solely responsible for your employment after the program. Your zeal is also measured by the consistency of your application to jobs outside the Stutern platform. While we provide career support, we expect our learners to be driven and tenacious about their careers.


What if I get the money for the tuition after the program but before I get the job?

We have different payment negotiations for our graduates with this scenario. It majorly depends on how long after graduation you are proposing to make this payment. Our account team is always ready to help out.


Can I switch my payment plan along the line?

Of course, you can. We do not control what the universe has in store for us.


What if I want to complete my financial obligation within a shorter time?

24 months might seem like a big deal, so, for some of us who can’t wait, our account team will be able to reach a compromise with a payment plan customized just for you. All of these, however, depend on a plethora of factors.


Class Model & Schedule Questions

Are the classes physical or virtual?

Our classes are 100% online which enables you to be a part of the class wherever you are. That said, we have a workspace in Gbagada, Lagos. This is available for enrolled learners who need access to the internet, power supply, and sometimes, the community of other learners.


What days of the week do classes hold?

Classes hold twice a week. The specific days are dependant on the course you are enrolled.


How flexible is the program?

Since our classes are online and you don’t have to be physically present, it is somewhat flexible. However, there are clear schedules to follow, starting with classes holding twice in a week and some other activities holding for about 1–2 hours on non-class days. Also, the class is focused on hands-on learning through projects. Thus, you will actively work on said projects on non-class days. Bottom line, it is a very immersive program.


I work full time. What options do I have?

Although the program isn’t on a self-paced model, we have recordings for live sessions to help you catch up. We however emphasize that learners make at least 95% of live sessions so that you have the chance to ask your tutor questions and get clarity in real time.


Are there any offline activities at all?

Yes, there are. Aside from the online classes, we hold team-building exercises and learners are free to join within a physical space. Depending on the location you are, we coordinate fellow learners together to have their meetups. Likewise, your instructors, teaching assistant, or guest instructors might also suggest offline sessions to foster collaboration among learners when necessary.


How would theses offline activities work for those online?

If you can’t make it to the physical space, you can still join offline sessions virtually.


Graduation Questions

Are there prerequisites for graduation?

Yes, a learner must complete at least 95% attendance of live sessions (including guest sessions and peer presentations), and 100% class tasks and projects.


Will certificates be given after the program is completed successfully?

Our focus is to work with you to build a portfolio of projects that can attract potential employers. However, we can make provisions to issue your certificate if the need arises before getting a job.


Job Placement Questions

Is a job guaranteed for me after Stutern?

We wish we could give you a definite yes but we don't promise our learners jobs. Our promise is to make you employable and connect you with employers. We will work with you to build a great portfolio that will give you the confidence to apply to your dream job. We will also provide you with career advisory services that include building a resume review, online profile review, interview preparation, job negotiation insights, etc.


Do I have to take the first job I’m offered?

Like we always say, we can’t force an employer on an Stutern graduate, neither can we force a Stutern graduate on an employer. However, we will be there to provide insights while you deliberate accepting offers. What’s most important is that you prioritize offers that pose room for career growth. Also, since salary raises are relatively quick in this field, we recommend that you don’t overrun your job search.


What happens if I don’t get a job after graduation?

If you opt for the ISA payment option, your obligation doesn’t start until you get a job (See “Tuition & Income Share Agreement (ISA) Questions” for more details). Regardless of your payment option, we will have your availability status set to “Actively searching” on the Stutern hire page and we will include your profile in placement marketing campaigns until the situation changes. We will work with you to improve your portfolio if there’s a need for that but we expect that you will continuously work on this improvement on your end as well.


Does Stutern get internship opportunities to help their learners gain real life experience?

Yes. As part of the project weeks' activities toward the tail of the program, there is typically a one-month client’s project also known as an internship. You will typically be grouped in teams alongside learners from either your track or other tracks. Employers then select teams to intern with their team on real-life projects.


Is the internship a paid internship?

Hmm, this isn’t where the big bucks come in. Employers typically provide stipends to cover transportation and feeding if it’s a physical engagement or data expenses if it’s a remote engagement.
